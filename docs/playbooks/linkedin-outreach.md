# LinkedIn Outreach Playbook

**Cadence:** 10 personalized DMs per day, M-F (50/week)
**Time required:** ~60-90 min per day, in evening hours
**Conversion target:** 5-10% reply rate → 2-3 discovery calls/week → 1 close/week (Phase 1)

---

## The funnel

```
100 cold DMs sent
    ↓ ~10% reply (genuine interest signal)
10 Loom audits delivered
    ↓ ~30% book a call
3 discovery calls held
    ↓ ~40% receive a proposal
1.2 proposals sent
    ↓ ~40% close
0.5 clients/week signed → 2 clients/month at steady state
```

In Phase 1 you're optimizing for the top of this funnel. Bad DMs = no replies = no clients.

## Pre-DM research (3-5 min per prospect)

For each prospect:

1. **Open their company website.** Look for:
   - Services offered (note specific specialties)
   - Service area
   - Any "lead capture" forms or chat widgets
   - Phone number visibility
   - Whether they list business hours
   - Project galleries / recent work

2. **Search them on Google Maps / Google Business Profile.** Look for:
   - Review count (more reviews = bigger operation)
   - Response patterns to reviews (do they respond? how fast?)
   - Phone number, address, hours displayed
   - Photos uploaded recently?

3. **Find ONE specific observation** to anchor your DM. Examples:
   - "Saw your work on the [specific project type] gallery"
   - "Noticed you've been in business [N] years in [city]"
   - "Saw you don't have a chat widget on the contact page — that's actually most of why I'm reaching out"

This 3-5 min of research is the single highest-leverage activity in outreach. Lazy DMs get ignored. Personalized DMs get replies.

## The DM template

```
Hey [First Name],

I run the AI ops at SEMCO Surfaces here in Las Vegas. We cut missed-lead loss to near zero last year using a system I built in-house.

Saw [Company Name] and noticed [SPECIFIC OBSERVATION]. Recorded a 5-min Loom showing what I found and where I think the biggest leak might be:

[Loom link]

No pitch in it — just an audit. If anything resonates, calendar's here: [Calendly]

Andy
```

Why each part matters:

- **"I run the AI ops at SEMCO Surfaces"** — credibility, real-world, not theoretical
- **"Las Vegas"** — local-ish to most NV/AZ/SoCal prospects, builds rapport
- **"We cut missed-lead loss to near zero"** — outcome, not feature
- **"Saw [Company Name]"** — proves you didn't mass-DM
- **"specific observation"** — this is the magic word
- **"5-min Loom showing what I found"** — value upfront, no ask
- **"No pitch in it — just an audit"** — disarms sales-objection
- **"Calendar's here"** — only one CTA, no hard sell

## Recording the Loom audit

5 minutes max. Structure:

**0:00-0:30 — Opening**
- Your face on camera in the corner
- "Hey [first name], Andy here from SEMCO Surfaces, recorded this just for you about [Company Name]."
- ONE genuine compliment ("First, your gallery is great — the [specific job type] work especially.")

**0:30-1:30 — Problem #1**
- Share screen, navigate to their website
- Point out specific issue with lead intake
- Common ones: no chat widget, slow page load, no clear CTA, contact form buried

**1:30-2:30 — Problem #2**
- Share screen, navigate to their Google Business Profile
- Point out: missed-call patterns, slow response to reviews, missing hours, etc.

**2:30-3:30 — Problem #3**
- Speculate (carefully) about their phone responsiveness
- "If you're like most contractors at this scale, I'd bet you're missing 15-25 calls a week, especially after hours and weekends."

**3:30-4:30 — The quantified leak**
- "Even at 10 missed calls/week, if your average ticket is $4,500 and you close 30%, that's $585K/year leaking out. Probably higher."
- Stop and let that number land.

**4:30-5:00 — Soft close**
- "I built a system at SEMCO that handles all three of these. Not pitching it now — just wanted to show you the gap. If you want to dig in, my calendar's in the DM."

**Tips:**
- Record on Loom (free tier is fine)
- Use the "auto-cleanup" feature to remove "ums" if you want
- Don't redo more than once — slight roughness reads as authentic
- Title the video: "Quick audit for [Company Name] — Andy"

## Daily routine

Time-boxed evening session, 60-90 min:

**Mon-Thu evening (7-8:30pm PT):**
- 30 min: research 10 new prospects, add to spreadsheet
- 30 min: record 10 Looms (3 min per record + 1 min upload buffer)
- 15 min: send 10 DMs with Loom links
- 15 min: handle replies from yesterday's DMs

**Friday evening:**
- Optional: catch-up day, plan next week's prospect list

**Saturday morning (8-11am PT):**
- Discovery calls only (3-4 max)

## Reply handling

When you get a reply, respond within 4 hours of seeing it (during your available windows).

**Positive reply patterns:**

> "Yeah this is interesting, tell me more"
**Reply with:** "Happy to. Easiest is a quick 25-min call this Saturday morning or [day] evening — calendar here: [Calendly]. If you'd rather, I can send a 1-page write-up of how it would work for your business."

> "We have someone else for this"
**Reply with:** "Totally fair. Mind sharing who you're using? Always learning what's working out there. If anything changes in 6-12 months, you have my contact."

> "Send me more info"
**Reply with:** "Sure thing — sent you my one-pager: [link]. Most useful is a 25-min call though, since I can tailor what I show you. Calendar: [Calendly]. Either way."

**Negative reply patterns:**

> "Not interested"
**Reply with:** Just "Appreciate the response. Best of luck, [first name]." Move on. Don't argue.

> "I don't need AI"
**Reply with:** "Fair. The product is automation more than AI specifically. If lead recovery isn't a priority for you right now, totally hear that. Happy to circle back in 6 months."

> "Take me off your list"
**Reply with:** Single word "Done." Remove from list immediately.

## Follow-up sequence (for no response)

After initial DM with no reply:

**Day 3 follow-up:**
> "Hey [first name] — bumping in case the Loom got buried. No pressure. Here's the link again if useful: [Loom]"

**Day 7 follow-up:**
> "Last note — I know you're busy. If timing's bad, totally understand. I'll close the file on this for now. You have my contact if it ever becomes relevant."

After Day 7, stop. Re-engage only on a clear trigger (they post about a relevant pain, time passes meaningfully, you have new social proof).

## What to NEVER do

- **Mass-DM without personalization.** Better to send 5 personalized DMs than 50 templates.
- **Pitch in the first message.** The Loom is value; the DM is a soft offer to talk.
- **Reuse the same Loom for multiple prospects.** Hyper-specific is the point.
- **Argue with "not interested."** It poisons the well for if they re-engage later.
- **Mention SEMCO competitors directly** by name. Protect the day job.
- **Promise specific outcomes you can't quantify yet.** "25%+ recovery" is the brand promise; don't go further (e.g., "we'll get you 50 leads/month") without data.

## Metrics to track weekly

In your prospect spreadsheet, calculate:

- DMs sent this week
- Reply rate (positive + negative)
- Calls booked
- Calls held
- Proposals sent
- Closes

If reply rate <5%, the DMs aren't personalized enough or the Loom isn't compelling. Fix that before sending more.
If reply rate >10% but no calls book, the Calendly link or follow-up game is the issue.
If calls book but don't close, the discovery script or proposal is the issue.

The funnel diagnoses itself if you actually look at the numbers.
