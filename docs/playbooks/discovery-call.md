# Discovery Call Playbook

**Duration:** 25 minutes (hard cap; book 30, end at 25)
**Goal:** Determine LLR vs SIA fit, get qualified pain points, build trust, set up the proposal
**When to schedule:** Saturday 8-11am PT preferred; weekday evening 5:30-7pm PT acceptable

---

## Pre-call prep (10 min)

Before every discovery call:

1. **Open their website.** Scroll the homepage, the services page, any project galleries.
2. **Search them on Google Maps.** Note reviews count, response patterns, photos.
3. **Check their LinkedIn presence** (the contact's and the company's).
4. **Note 3 specific observations** you can reference in the first 5 minutes:
   - Project type that caught your eye
   - Service area
   - Years in business
   - Notable client work
5. **Open the discovery script in another tab.**

## Call structure (5 sections, ~5 min each)

### Section 1 — Business snapshot (rapport + qualification)

Open with one of your prepared observations. Then:

- "How long have you been in business?"
- "What's your typical job size — average ticket?"
- "How are most of your leads coming in right now — phone, web, referrals, Google?"

What you're listening for:
- Revenue band (qualifies them in/out: $1M-$10M sweet spot)
- Ticket size (informs ROI math)
- Lead source mix (signals which problems exist)

### Section 2 — Lead flow questions (signals for LLR)

- "How many calls would you estimate you miss per week?"
- "When a lead comes in after hours, what happens to it?"
- "How fast do you typically respond to a new inquiry?"
- "What's your rough close rate on inbound leads?"
- "Who handles initial lead intake — you, an office manager, sales?"

What you're listening for:
- 10+ missed calls/week = strong LLR signal
- "After hours, it goes to voicemail and we get to it Monday" = strong LLR signal
- Response time >30 min = strong LLR signal
- Owner doing intake personally = LLR signal (saves them time too)

### Section 3 — Operations questions (signals for SIA)

- "Walk me through what happens after someone says 'yes, send me a quote.'"
- "How do you track who's been quoted, who's pending, who's booked?"
- "Where does customer info live — CRM, spreadsheet, paper, email threads?"
- "How much time does [the admin person] spend on data entry and status updates per week?"
- "What's the most repetitive task in your week?"

What you're listening for:
- Multiple disconnected systems = SIA signal
- "Excel + email + texts" = SIA signal (Module A: Lead-to-CRM Pipeline)
- 15+ hours/week of admin = SIA signal
- "I have to look up product specs constantly" = SIA Module C signal (Knowledge Engine)

### Section 4 — Tech inventory (scoping)

- "What software do you currently use? CRM, accounting, scheduling, anything else?"
- "Does your website capture leads, or is it just informational?"
- "Are you on Shopify, Squarespace, WordPress, or custom?"

What you're listening for:
- They have GoHighLevel, HubSpot, Monday → adapter exists, fast onboarding
- They have Jobber/ServiceTitan → generic webhook config needed
- They have nothing → upsell to Module A (give them a CRM)

### Section 5 — Decision close

- "If we could [their specific pain], what would that be worth to you per month?"
- "Who else is involved in a decision like this?"
- "What's your timeline — this month, this quarter, just exploring?"

What you're listening for:
- They quantify pain in dollars → easy close
- Sole decision-maker → fast close
- Timeline within 30 days → priority follow-up
- "Just exploring" → lower-priority follow-up, longer cadence

## Pitch decision matrix

After the call, decide what to send:

| Dominant pain signal | Send |
|---|---|
| 10+ missed calls/week, slow follow-up, after-hours leak | **LLR proposal** ($1,500-$2,500 setup + $397-$597/mo) |
| Decent lead handling, owner doing 15+ hrs/week of admin | **SIA paid audit** ($497-$997) |
| Customer info scattered across email/text/notes | **SIA Module C audit + proposal** |
| High-ticket jobs ($5K+) with low conversion | **LLR proposal** with conversion-focused angle |
| Multiple disconnected tools, manual data entry between them | **SIA Core audit** ($997) |
| Both lead AND ops problems | **LLR proposal first** (faster ROI); pre-frame SIA as Phase 2 |
| They balk at $597/mo before you even quote | Disqualify gracefully, suggest a self-serve tool |
| Direct SEMCO competitor in NV/AZ | Decline to work with them (protect day job) |

## Disqualification scripts

Disqualifying gracefully matters. Never argue them into a deal they can't afford.

**Too small (under $500K revenue):**
> "Sounds like you're early in your growth. Honestly, what I do is meaningful at your stage but the price point is going to feel steep. I'd recommend [free or low-cost tool] for now. Reach back out when you cross $1M — happy to help then."

**Wrong fit:**
> "Based on what you described, I don't think I'm the right person for this. Sounds more like [other category of solution]. Want me to point you at a couple of options that might fit better?"

**Bad gut feel (they were rude, dismissive, or seem high-maintenance):**
> "Appreciate you taking the time. I think this might not be the right fit on our end — we're being selective about who we onboard right now. Best of luck with [their goal]."

You're not desperate for clients. Disqualifying a bad fit protects every future case study, NPS score, and referral.

## What to send within 24 hours of the call

Either:

1. **The proposal** (Notion link), if it was an LLR fit
2. **The paid audit offer** ($497-$997), if it was an SIA fit
3. **A polite "no thanks" note**, if they were disqualified

Either way: send something within 24 hours. Momentum matters. Cold proposals from 5 days ago die.

## Post-call notes (always)

After every call, write a 1-paragraph summary in your CRM:

```
[Date] - Discovery with [Name], [Business]
Revenue band: $X-$Y
Pain signals: [LLR/SIA/both/none]
Decision timeline: [this month/quarter/exploring]
Followup: [proposal sent / audit offered / disqualified]
Notes: [anything specific they said that should inform the proposal or future content]
```

These notes become content gold. Real contractor quotes about real pains are LinkedIn fuel for 6 months.
