# Marketing Copy

External writing follows specific rules to maintain brand coherence and conversion velocity. Use this skill whenever writing for prospects, clients, or public audience.

## Positioning anchor

Repeat this single positioning at every touchpoint:

> **TraceFlow is AI Lead Recovery for surface, countertop, flooring, and pool resurfacing contractors. We recover 25%+ of your missed-call revenue within 30 days, or your first month is free.**

The Software Integration & Automation (SIA) offering exists but is NEVER marketed externally. It's an internal expansion path for existing clients or self-identified ops-pain prospects. **Marketing always leads with LLR.**

Why this discipline matters: a focused, niched message converts. A diffuse "we do AI stuff for contractors" message doesn't. Resist the temptation to widen the funnel.

## Voice

TraceFlow's voice is:
- **Direct.** No fluff. Contractors hate marketing-speak.
- **Confident, not boastful.** "We recover 25%+ of missed revenue" — not "We can help you maybe recover some leads if everything aligns!"
- **Specific.** Numbers, timeframes, named systems. Never "leverage" or "synergies."
- **Slightly mechanical.** Contractor-friendly tonality. Tools, systems, hands-on. Not "AI-powered transformation."
- **Founder-led.** Andy speaks. Real voice. Real SEMCO experience. Not "our team."

Things to avoid in copy:
- "Empower," "leverage," "ecosystem," "synergize," "best-in-class," "world-class"
- Emojis in B2B writing
- Excessive em-dashes mid-sentence (use parentheses or new sentences)
- "We help you..." as an opener (use "We do X" — the active form)
- Vague timeframes ("quickly," "fast") — use real numbers ("within 30 days")
- Hedging ("might," "could," "potentially") — assert outcomes; back with ROI math

## LinkedIn outreach DM template

```
Subject: Quick note about [Business Name]

Hey [First Name],

I run the AI ops at SEMCO Surfaces here in Las Vegas. We cut missed-lead loss to near zero last year using a system I built in-house.

Saw [Business Name] and noticed [specific observation about their site / Google profile / response speed]. Recorded a 5-min Loom showing what I found and where I think the biggest leak is:

[Loom link]

No pitch in it — just an audit. If anything resonates, calendar's here:
[Calendly link]

Andy
```

Personalization required for every DM. The "specific observation" is non-negotiable — it's what makes this not feel like spam.

## Loom audit script

The 5-minute Loom is the highest-converting outreach asset. Structure:

1. **0:00-0:30 — Open** with their business name and a specific compliment ("Saw your work on the kitchen remodel page — solid craftsmanship.")
2. **0:30-1:30 — Identify Problem #1** with their digital lead intake. Show your screen with their site or Google profile. Specific.
3. **1:30-2:30 — Identify Problem #2.** Usually phone responsiveness or after-hours.
4. **2:30-3:30 — Identify Problem #3.** Usually CRM or follow-up.
5. **3:30-4:30 — Quantify the cost.** "If you're missing even 10 calls a week at your average ticket, that's $X/month leaking out."
6. **4:30-5:00 — Close.** "Happy to walk through how I'd fix this. Calendar's in the DM."

Don't make them watch 10 minutes. Don't pitch. Diagnose only.

## The SEMCO case study

This is your single most important written asset. Structure:

**1-page web version:**

```
[Logo + business name]

THE CHALLENGE
SEMCO Surfaces was losing 30+ leads/month to missed after-hours calls and slow CRM follow-up. Sales team spent 12+ hours/week on manual lead entry.

THE SYSTEM
Built a Shopify → Monday.com pipeline with AI-assisted qualification. Connected to a 193-entry knowledge base auto-extracted from product TDS sheets and install guides.

THE RESULTS
- [X]% increase in qualified leads captured
- [Y] hours/week saved on manual entry
- [$Z] in attributed pipeline recovered

[Pull quote from someone at SEMCO if usable]
```

**2-page PDF version:** same content + screenshots, timeline, technical sidebar, larger pull quote, branded design.

Until real metrics are captured, this is "in progress" copy — placeholders until you nail the numbers down. **Don't publish until numbers are real.** Made-up numbers compound trust loss.

## Proposal template (Notion)

After a discovery call, send a Notion proposal within 24 hours. Sections:

1. **Cover.** "Proposal for [Business Name] — AI Lead Recovery System"
2. **What you told me.** Bulleted recap of their stated problem (proves you listened)
3. **What we'll build.** 3-5 bullets matching their pain
4. **What it'll cost.** Setup + monthly. No ranges. No "starting at."
5. **What you'll get back.** ROI math: "If we recover even [X] leads/month at your $[Y] average ticket, that's $[Z]/mo back. Pays for itself in [N] weeks."
6. **Timeline.** Concrete dates. "Kickoff this Friday, soft launch by [date], full launch by [date]."
7. **Guarantee.** First month money-back if no measurable recovery.
8. **Next step.** "Reply 'go' and I'll send the contract. 50% deposit invoiced on signing."

Keep it under 800 words. Long proposals lose to short ones.

## Email cadence after proposal

| Day | Subject | Body |
|---|---|---|
| Day 2 | "Quick follow-up on the TraceFlow proposal" | "Wanted to make sure this didn't get buried. Any questions I can answer?" |
| Day 5 | "Thinking about it from your angle" | One paragraph of empathy — they're busy, capital is finite, etc. Soft pressure: "Happy to revise the scope if helpful." |
| Day 10 | "Last note from me" | "Going to close the file on this for now. If timing changes, you know where to find me. Will leave the proposal active for 30 days." |

Then stop. No more emails. Re-engage only on a clear trigger (they reply, they post about a pain point, time passes meaningfully).

## Voice samples — DO and DON'T

**DON'T:**
> "TraceFlow empowers home service businesses to leverage cutting-edge AI to optimize their lead management ecosystem and unlock unprecedented growth."

This says nothing. Generic. Slop.

**DO:**
> "Your phone rings. Nobody answers. That lead never calls back. We fix that within 30 days."

Specific. Active. Asserts an outcome.

**DON'T:**
> "We're excited to partner with contractors on their digital transformation journey."

Salesforce talk. Cringe.

**DO:**
> "I built this at SEMCO Surfaces. Now I run it for other surface contractors who can't afford to keep losing leads."

Founder voice. Concrete. Trustworthy.

## When to use AI to draft vs write yourself

- **Use AI to draft:** landing page sections, LinkedIn post first drafts, proposal sections, email follow-ups, ad copy variants
- **Always edit yourself:** anything with a specific number, specific client, or specific commitment. Don't let AI hallucinate metrics.
- **Write from scratch:** outreach DMs to specific prospects (personalization is the conversion driver), case studies (judgment-heavy), important client emails

When using AI to draft: paste the brief + the voice samples above + the positioning anchor. Ask for 3 variants. Edit aggressively. AI's first drafts are 60% of the way there; the last 40% is taste.
